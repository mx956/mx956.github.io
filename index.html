About Me

Ruixuan (Matt) Xie is a MASc student dedicated to advancing the field of astrophysics through cutting-edge digital signal processing (DSP). His work focuses on enhancing the readout capabilities of the Fred Young Submillimeter Telescope (FYST), located at an altitude of 5600 meters and equipped with a record-breaking number of pixels.

Research

By utilizing a novel DSP technique known as polyphase synthesis filter bank (PSB), Matt's research enables more efficient and dynamic detection of cosmic phenomena, allowing the telescope to produce high-resolution images of space with unprecedented detail and clarity. His innovations in bandwidth and pixel count enhancement facilitate real-time, dynamic adjustments in detector tracking, significantly advancing our understanding of Big Bang Cosmology, star and galaxy formation, and other key areas in astrophysics. Matt's contributions not only enhance the capabilities of the FYST but also set a new standard for future telescope technologies.
